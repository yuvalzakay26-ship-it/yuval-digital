# Projects — Showcase Strategy

The projects section is a critical trust signal. Because Yuval is at the start of his path as an independent builder, the section must be **fully honest** about what is real client work and what is concept demonstration. Misleading visitors here would destroy the brand we are trying to earn.

## Showcase Principles

1. **Honesty before showmanship.** Every entry is labeled clearly: `Real project` or `Concept demo`.
2. **One real flagship.** Today the only "real" project is `yuval.digital` itself — the personal brand site this repository builds. As real client work ships, it replaces concept entries one at a time.
3. **Concepts must look concept-quality, not fake-history.** A concept demo is a *thought experiment* about how Yuval would approach a problem — not a fabricated case study with fake metrics.
4. **No fabricated outcomes.** Real outcomes only appear once they exist and the client approves.
5. **Bilingual parity.** Every project has both Hebrew and English versions of name, category, and description.

## Visual Treatment of Real vs. Concept

- The cover chip is **green-dotted "Real project"** for real work and **amber-dotted "Concept demo"** for concepts.
- Concept cards show a faint diagonal hatch pattern in the body to reinforce the distinction at a glance.
- Both share the same premium typography, motion and finish — concept demos are *not* lower quality, only *not yet client work*.

## Replacement Policy

As real client work ships:

1. The earliest concept is replaced by a real case study.
2. The replaced concept can be retired, or kept on a future `/concepts` page.
3. The "Real project" count grows naturally; we never inflate it.

## Confidentiality Policy

- Every real project is described in outcome terms; client name appears only after written approval.
- Commercial figures appear only as percentages or order-of-magnitude — never absolute, unless approved.
- Concept demos do not need confidentiality clearance because they describe no real client.
