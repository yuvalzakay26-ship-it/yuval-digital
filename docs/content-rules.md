# Content Rules

Every word on the site is held to the same engineering standard as the code. This document is the authoritative content rulebook.

## Core Rules

1. **Write for both languages independently.** Hebrew is not translated from English, and English is not translated from Hebrew. Every string is composed natively, with full awareness of cultural rhythm and idiom in that language.
2. **One idea per sentence.** No nested clauses; no parenthetical asides.
3. **Outcome before mechanism.** Lead with what changes for the reader, then explain how it works.
4. **Ban fluff.** No "in today's world…", no "we live in an era…", no "leverage cutting-edge…".
5. **Verbs over adjectives.** A premium brand earns adjectives; it does not declare them.
6. **No emojis in product copy.** Site copy is silent and confident.
7. **Specificity over scale.** Concrete numbers (`reduced ops time by ~60%`) beat vague claims (`drastically reduced overhead`).

## Hebrew Voice Rules

- שפה גבוהה אך לא מתנשאת.
- ללא תרגומיות מאנגלית — אם משפט נשמע מתורגם, נכתב מחדש.
- שמות מותגים לועזיים יישארו לטיניים (`Claude`, `n8n`, `WhatsApp`) — לא תעתיק.
- מספרים מקובלים בעברית: שיעורים באחוזים, סכומים בשקלים.
- ללא קלישאות "פתרונות חדשניים", "עידן הדיגיטל", "פורצי דרך".

## English Voice Rules

- US English by default. Avoid British spellings unless a client requires them.
- Sentence case in headings except for proper nouns.
- Em dash with no spaces — like this. Not " - ".
- Smart quotes only.
- Active voice always.

## Structural Patterns

### Section pattern
`Eyebrow` → `Title` → `Lead` → `Body`. Every section. No exceptions.

### Service card pattern
`Icon` → `Title` → `Description (1–2 sentences)` → `Bullets (4 max)`.

### Project card pattern
`Cover (visual)` → `Category` → `Title` → `Description (1 sentence)` → `CTA`.

### Hero pattern
`Eyebrow (role triple)` → `Two-line title (lead + highlight)` → `Subtitle (60ch max)` → `Two CTAs` → `Three metrics` → `Scroll hint`.

## CTA Library

All CTAs are short, action-led, in first-person plural where natural.

### Hebrew
- `בואו נבנה משהו אמיתי` — primary, hero
- `הצגת השירותים` — secondary
- `התחלת פרויקט` — nav CTA
- `שליחת בקשה` — form

### English
- `Let's build something real` — primary, hero
- `Explore services` — secondary
- `Start a project` — nav CTA
- `Send request` — form

## Vocabulary Bans

Never appear on the site, in any language:

- "synergy"
- "cutting-edge"
- "best-in-class"
- "next-generation"
- "revolutionary"
- "world-class"
- "unique value proposition"
- "פורץ דרך"
- "פתרונות חכמים בשבילך"
- "העתיד כבר כאן"

## Tone By Section

- **Hero:** confident statement of identity. No hedging.
- **Services:** pragmatic, outcome-driven, specific.
- **About:** first-person, warm but precise.
- **Projects:** evidence-mode. Outcomes only.
- **Contact:** inviting and direct. The reader has decided to write — make it easy.

## Editing Checklist

Before any string ships:

- [ ] Could a senior engineer defend every claim?
- [ ] Could a non-technical buyer understand it instantly?
- [ ] Is it under 60 characters for headings?
- [ ] Is it under 60 words for paragraphs?
- [ ] Is the parallel-language version equally strong?
- [ ] Does it avoid every banned phrase?
